# Forgery Simulator

> An open-world crime simulation game set in Southeast Asia, blending production systems, economic strategy, asset growth, and narrative-driven downfall.

---

## Overview

**Forgery Simulator** is an open-world simulation game set in the fictional Southeast Asian city **Siem Muang**, located in the fictional country **Prey Veng**.

The player takes the role of **Anand**, a young man who enters the underground economy to pay for his mother’s medical treatment. What begins as small-scale illegal printing gradually evolves into a larger criminal network involving fake documents, counterfeit goods, black market trading, asset ownership, money laundering, gang conflict, and cross-border crime.

The game is designed as a narrative-driven simulation where every system pushes the player toward expansion, risk, and eventual consequences.

## Core Concept

Forgery Simulator combines:

- production simulation
- resource gathering
- black market trading
- money laundering progression
- asset management
- gang relationship systems
- law enforcement pressure
- narrative consequences

The player starts with almost nothing and slowly develops a criminal operation. However, the larger the operation becomes, the more attention it attracts from police, financial investigators, and eventually international authorities.

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

```text
Acquire Materials → Produce Fake Goods → Sell or Launder → Buy Assets → Recruit People → Expand Operations → Manage Risk → Trigger Investigation
```

The main gameplay loop is built around risk and reward:

1. Collect or purchase materials.
2. Manufacture counterfeit goods.
3. Sell products through contacts or black market orders.
4. Launder fake money through shops, markets, businesses, and later financial companies.
5. Buy warehouses and factories.
6. Recruit workers and gang members.
7. Expand production and influence.
8. Avoid police and financial investigations.
9. Eventually face unavoidable collapse or moral reckoning.

## Story Background

### Prey Veng

**Prey Veng** is a fictional Southeast Asian country inspired by Cambodia and Laos. It has a rich cultural background, traditional architecture, rural landscapes, temples, markets, rivers, and a developing urban economy.

The country is undergoing modernization, but corruption, black market activity, and underground industries remain deeply rooted in some cities.

### Siem Muang

**Siem Muang** is the third-largest city in Prey Veng and an important development zone. It is inspired by the atmosphere of Siem Reap.

The city includes:

- a compact urban center
- traditional residential districts
- industrial warehouses and factories
- busy markets
- farmland
- forests
- rivers and bridges
- an ancient temple complex inspired by Angkor-style ruins

Siem Muang is both a cultural city and a hidden center of black market activity.

## Main Character

### Anand

Anand is a young man from Siem Muang. His family is poor, and his mother is seriously ill. When hospital bills become impossible to pay, Anand is pulled into the underground world by his uncle.

Anand is not initially a hardened criminal. His descent begins from desperation, family pressure, and the belief that he has no other option.

## Main Mission Structure

The main story contains **21 missions**.

### Mission 1 – Hospital Bill (Chapter 1)

Anand visits the hospital and learns that his mother needs urgent treatment. The medical cost becomes the emotional trigger for the entire game.

### Mission 2 – Uncle’s Secret (Chapter 1)

Anand visits his uncle, a printing factory employee. The uncle reveals that he can help Anand obtain basic illegal printing materials.

### Mission 3 – First Attempt (Chapter 1)

The player learns the basic production interface and produces the first small batch of low-quality 5-denomination fake notes.

### Mission 4 – First Deal (Chapter 1)

Anand delivers the first batch to Vik, the black market contact. This introduces the black market and trust system.

### Mission 5 – Small Orders (Chapter 2)

Vik gives Anand several small jobs to test reliability. The player completes basic fake money orders.

### Mission 6 – Basic Laundering (Chapter 2)

The player learns that small fake notes can be spent at convenience stores and small shops. The mission requires successfully laundering 10 small fake notes.

### Mission 7 – Market Access (Chapter 2)

The market area unlocks. The player gains access to vendors selling basic paper, herbs, food, drinks, medicine, and ordinary chemicals.

### Mission 8 – Better Quality (Chapter 3)

The player upgrades production quality using mid-tier paper materials. This mission introduces the crafting quality system.

### Mission 9 – Fake Documents (Chapter 3)

The player unlocks fake document orders through a computer-based job board. Fake IDs and driving-license-style documents become available.

### Mission 10 – Bigger Deals (Chapter 3)

Anand completes a larger fake money batch and begins to attract stronger criminal attention.

### Mission 11 – First Warehouse (Chapter 4)

The player purchases the first warehouse in the industrial zone. Asset ownership becomes available.

### Mission 12 – Recruit Workers (Chapter 4)

The player recruits two workers to improve production efficiency. Worker management is introduced.

### Mission 13 – Stable Production (Chapter 4)

The player must maintain continuous production while managing materials, suspicion, and cash flow.

### Mission 14 – Gang Recognition (Chapter 5)

A local gang leader recognizes Anand’s value and offers protection in exchange for cooperation.

### Mission 15 – Territory Conflict (Chapter 5)

The player becomes involved in local gang conflict. This mission introduces territory pressure and intimidation-style tasks.

### Mission 16 – Factory Setup (Chapter 5)

The player purchases the first factory and upgrades from small-scale production to industrial production.

### Mission 17 – Automation Unlock (Chapter 6)

After reaching advanced manufacturing, the player unlocks automated printing equipment.

### Mission 18 – High-End Production (Chapter 6)

The player produces high-value fake currency using advanced materials and more complex production systems.

### Mission 19 – Bribe Officials (Chapter 7)

The player uses influence and money to reduce local investigation pressure. This introduces corruption mechanics.

### Mission 20 – Financial Network (Chapter 7)

The player launders large sums through businesses and financial front companies.

### Mission 21 – International Deal (Chapter 8)

The player completes a cross-border deal. This triggers foreign financial detection and starts the unavoidable endgame investigation.

## Endings

### Ending 1 – Total Loss

Anand’s operation collapses. His assets are seized, his contacts disappear, and he escapes Siem Muang with only 500 dollars left.

### Ending 2 – Moral Realization

Anand realizes the damage caused by his actions and turns himself in. He still receives long-term imprisonment due to the severity of his crimes.

### Ending 3 – Arrest

Anand fails to escape the investigation and is arrested. His factories, warehouses, and financial fronts are exposed.

### Post-Ending Open World Mode

After certain endings, the player may continue in a reduced open-world state. Assets may be lost, contacts may disappear, and the player must rebuild under harsher conditions.

## Production System

Production is designed as a fictional, non-operational game mechanic. It does not represent real-world methods.

### Production Stages

1. Material acquisition
2. Material processing
3. Production quality check
4. Batch completion
5. Delivery, sale, or laundering

### Quality Factors

Product quality affects:

- detection chance
- sale value
- customer satisfaction
- police suspicion
- financial investigation risk

## Counterfeit Money Progression

The player begins with only low-value notes and gradually unlocks larger denominations through experience and equipment upgrades.

### Progression

| Stage | Unlock |
|---|---|
| Early Game | 5 denomination notes |
| Early-Mid Game | 10 denomination notes |
| Mid Game | 50 and 100 denomination notes |
| Late Game | 500 denomination notes |
| Endgame | 1000 denomination notes |

Higher denominations generate more profit but are easier to detect and create larger investigation pressure.

## Paper Crafting System

Paper quality is one of the main progression systems.

### Basic Paper

Used for low-value fake notes.

- cotton fiber paper
- basic ink effect
- low quality
- low detection risk only when used in small amounts

### Mid-Tier Paper

Used for medium-value notes.

- cotton fiber
- linen-based material
- improved ink effect
- better durability

### Advanced Paper

Used for high-value notes.

- cotton fiber
- linen-based material
- synthetic fiber
- high-strength paper
- special ink effect
- fictional enzyme component
- security strip

Advanced materials are harder to acquire and mostly require black market access.

## Black Market Merchant System

Black market merchants are not always available. They appear based on reputation, mission progress, and story stage.

### Black Market Exclusive Materials

- security strips
- color-shifting ink
- synthetic fiber paper
- advanced document kits
- restricted upgrade parts

### Black Market Rules

- limited stock
- higher prices
- reputation-based access
- risk of police traps or gang betrayal

## Money Laundering Development

Money laundering means converting fake money in the player’s possession into usable game currency or assets.

### Stage 1 – Convenience Stores

The player spends small-value fake notes in convenience stores.

Features:

- low risk
- low profit
- repeated use increases police attention
- if caught, Anand can claim he received the note from someone else

### Stage 2 – Local Markets

The player uses fake notes at local markets and small vendors.

Features:

- moderate risk
- wider coverage
- vendor familiarity affects suspicion

### Stage 3 – Goods Conversion

The player buys goods and resells them.

Features:

- converts fake cash into items
- reduces direct money trail
- requires time and contacts

### Stage 4 – Business Laundering

The player buys small businesses and runs illegal funds through them as game abstraction.

Features:

- higher capacity
- requires asset ownership
- produces regular income
- adds financial monitoring risk

### Stage 5 – Financial Company Laundering

The player establishes or controls financial front companies.

Features:

- high volume
- high risk
- requires bribery and business assets
- leads to financial investigation systems

### Stage 6 – Cross-Border Laundering

The player expands beyond Prey Veng.

Features:

- massive profit
- international detection risk
- triggers the late-game investigation arc

## Vehicle System

Recommended scope: motorcycle-first.

### Why Motorcycles

- lower development cost
- fits Southeast Asian city atmosphere
- easier navigation in narrow streets
- supports delivery missions

### Possible Later Expansion

- small trucks for factory logistics
- police vehicles for chase sequences

---

## Combat System

Recommended scope: limited melee combat.

### Design Direction

- avoid heavy gunplay
- focus on intimidation, escape, and close-range conflict
- use sticks, blunt weapons, and environmental threats

This keeps development manageable and prevents the game from becoming a full action shooter.

---

## CG and Narrative Presentation Strategy

To control budget, the game should avoid too many expensive CG scenes.

### Recommended Approach

- use in-engine cutscenes
- use dialogue boxes
- use camera pans
- use environmental storytelling
- use still-image story panels when needed

### Key Scenes Worth Cinematic Treatment

1. Hospital opening scene
2. Uncle reveals the illegal opportunity
3. First black market deal
4. First factory purchase
5. Cross-border deal
6. Final investigation sequence

Most other story beats should be handled through dialogue, phone calls, UI messages, and in-world events.

---

## Design Philosophy

Forgery Simulator is not designed as a power fantasy only. It is a rise-and-fall simulation.

The player gains money, power, and influence, but every expansion increases the chance of collapse.

### Core Design Principles

- Start small, scale gradually.
- Every profitable action creates risk.
- Systems should connect naturally.
- Story progression should emerge from player behavior.
- The ending should feel inevitable but player-shaped.

---

## Development Strategy

### Low-Budget Priorities

1. Build a small but dense map.
2. Focus on industrial zone, market, and residential area first.
3. Use modular buildings.
4. Use repeated interiors.
5. Use in-engine cutscenes instead of rendered CG.
6. Build systems before visual polish.
7. Start with motorcycles instead of full vehicle systems.

### First Prototype Scope

The first playable prototype should include:

- small residential area
- uncle interaction
- simple production table
- first fake note batch
- Vik delivery mission
- basic shop laundering
- police suspicion meter
